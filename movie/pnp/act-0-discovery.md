## Chapter 0.1 — The Recruiter

### Scene 0.1.1 — The LinkedIn Message
- Jun-seo is at his desk at Hyundai Robotics, 11 PM, debugging a kinematic solver
- LinkedIn notification: "Hi Jun-seo, I'm a recruiter at Sanctuary AI in Vancouver. We're building humanoid robots and your kinematics work at Hyundai is exactly what we need. Would you be open to a conversation?"
- Thinks: "Sanctuary AI. I've seen their demos — the Phoenix robot. Vancouver. That's... Canada."

### Scene 0.1.2 — Research Mode
- Jun-seo spends 3 nights researching:
  - Sanctuary AI — $100M+ funding, humanoid robots, Vancouver HQ
  - Canada immigration for tech workers — overwhelming, confusing
  - Express Entry — calculates his CRS: ~430. Recent draws at 480+. Not enough.
  - BC PNP Tech — "accelerated processing for tech workers with job offers"
  - Thinks: "My CRS is too low for Express Entry alone. But with a PNP nomination it goes to 1030. That's the play."

### Scene 0.1.3 — The Interview
- Video interviews with Sanctuary AI — 4 rounds over 2 weeks
- Technical: robot kinematics, ROS2, computer vision — Jun-seo excels
- Offer: $120K CAD + stock options, Robotics Software Engineer
- Condition: "pending work authorization in Canada"
- Sanctuary HR: "We can support you through BC PNP Tech. We recommend working with an immigration consultant. Have you heard of CanaDREAMERS?"

## Chapter 0.2 — Finding the Platform

### Scene 0.2.1 — CanaDREAMERS
