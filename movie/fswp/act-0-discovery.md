## Chapter 0.1 — The Gap

### Scene 0.1.1 — The Number
**Agent: Amara (narrated)**
- Amara is at her desk at Interswitch's Lagos office, 11th floor, Victoria Island
- She has two monitors: one showing a Kafka dashboard with 2 million transactions scrolling past, one showing a spreadsheet
- The spreadsheet is her life in numbers:
  - Salary: ₦18,000,000/year ($16K CAD)
  - Rent (Lekki apartment, shared): ₦3,600,000
  - Mother's upkeep (Enugu): ₦2,400,000
  - Emeka's medical school fees: ₦4,200,000
  - Savings this year: ₦1,800,000 ($1,600 CAD)
  - Total saved over 3 years: $25,000 CAD
- Thinks: "I build systems that move $4 billion a year. I earn less than a junior developer in Toronto makes in two months."

### Scene 0.1.2 — The Comparison
**Agent: Amara (narrated)**
- Opens LinkedIn. Scrolls past a post from Tunde — her classmate at UniLag
- Tunde moved to Toronto two years ago. Data engineer at Shopify. Posted a photo of his condo in Liberty Village
- His salary: $115,000 CAD. Seven times what Amara earns for the same work
- Below his post: "Grateful for this journey. Canada changed my life."
- Thinks: "Tunde was not smarter than me. He was not a better engineer. He just left."

### Scene 0.1.3 — The Conversation
**Agent: Amara (narrated)**

- Ngozi: "Amara, your cousin Adaeze is asking about her wedding contribution..."
- Amara sends money. She always sends money
- Ngozi: "When will you rest? You work too hard"

## Chapter 0.2 — The Research

### Scene 0.2.1 — The Express Entry Page
**Agent: Amara**
- Saturday morning. Amara opens Canada.ca on her laptop
- She has researched this before — she's been reading about Express Entry for 8 months
- She knows the system:
  - Create an Express Entry profile
  - Get a CRS score
  - Wait for a draw
  - If your score is above the cutoff, you get an ITA (Invitation to Apply)
  - Submit full application → permanent residence
- The problem: she's been watching the draw results
- Every draw she checks: "Minimum CRS: 524" ... "Minimum CRS: 509" ... "Minimum CRS: 491"
- Her CRS estimate: 470
- Thinks: "11 points short of the lowest draw I've seen. And those were STEM draws — not even general draws. There haven't been general draws in over a year."

### Scene 0.2.2 — The STEM Angle
**Agent: Amara**
- She reads about category-based selection — IRCC's targeted draws
- STEM draws specifically for science, technology, engineering, and math workers
- Her NOC: 21220 — Cybersecurity specialists. It's on the STEM list
- Recent STEM draws:
  - April 2024: CRS 491, 4,500 ITAs
  - December 2023: CRS 481, 5,900 ITAs
  - July 2023: CRS 486, 500 ITAs
- 481 is the lowest. She's at 470. Eleven points away
- Thinks: "Eleven points. That's it. Eleven points between this life and a different one."

### Scene 0.2.3 — The CRS Calculator
**Agent: Amara**
- She opens the CRS calculator for the hundredth time
- Enters her information:
  - Age: 31 → 99 points
  - Education: Master's → 135 points
  - English (IELTS 8.5): CLB 10 → 136 points
  - Foreign work experience (core): 0 points
  - Skill transferability → 100 points
  - Total: 470
- She's already taken IELTS twice. First time: 7.5. Second time: 8.5. She maxed out what she can get from English
- She's already 31. Next year she turns 32 and LOSES points
- Thinks: "The clock is running against me. Every birthday costs me CRS points. I need to be in the pool before I turn 32."

### Scene 0.2.4 — The French Idea
**Agent: Amara**
- Reads a forum post on canadavisa.com: "How I gained 37 CRS points with French"
- She learned French in secondary school. 6 years of it. She can read menus and ask for directions
- The TEF (Test d'évaluation de français) can give her additional CRS points
- CLB 7 in French: +37 CRS points through SOL points (12) and bilingual bonus (25)
- 470 + 37 = 507. Above every STEM cutoff she's seen
- Thinks: "Je peux faire ça. I can do this."
- But she needs to prepare. The TEF isn't easy. And she needs a consultant to make sure her application is bulletproof — one mistake and she waits another year

## Chapter 0.3 — Finding CanaDREAMERS

### Scene 0.3.1 — The Search
**Agent: Amara**
- Google: "verified immigration consultant Canada FSWP express entry"

### Scene 0.3.2 — The Platform
**Agent: Amara**
- Lands on CanaDREAMERS
- Sees: "Verified consultants only. Every consultant is CICC-registered."
- Browses consultant profiles
- Finds Chris Daw: RCIC R409583, Vancouver, specializations include FSWP
- Sees his fee range: $3,000-6,000
- Thinks: "That's 3 months of my rent. But Tunde's agent charged $5,000 and it worked. The difference is accountability. If Chris is on this platform and CICC-verified, he can't disappear with my money."
- She doesn't sign up yet. She takes a screenshot of Chris's profile and closes the tab
- She needs to think

### Scene 0.3.3 — The Decision
**Agent: Amara (narrated)**
- Sunday evening. Amara sits on the balcony of her Lekki apartment
- The Lagos skyline is orange with dust and sunset
- She opens the screenshot of Chris's profile
- She opens her bank app: $25,412 CAD saved
- She opens the CRS calculator one more time: 470
- She thinks about Tunde at Shopify. She thinks about her mother's roof. She thinks about Emeka's tuition
- Thinks: "I have been thinking about this for 8 months. The CRS gap is 11 points. The French strategy is real. The money is there. I am running out of birthdays."
- She opens CanaDREAMERS and clicks "Sign Up"
