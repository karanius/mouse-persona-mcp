## Chapter 3.2 — The Consultation

### Scene 3.2.1 — Video Call
**Both agents — narrated**
- 30-minute consultation
- Chris shares his screen — shows the CRS calculator
- Goes through Amara's score point by point, confirming:
  - Age: 31 → 99 (turns 32 on Jan 18, 2027 → drops to 94. She has 8 months)
  - Education: MSc → 135 (maximum with ECA ✅)
  - English: CLB 10 → 136 (can't improve — she's effectively maxed)
  - Foreign work experience (core): 0 (foreign work gets no core points for single applicant)
  - Skill transferability: 100 (education×language 50 + work×language 50)
  - Total: 470

- Chris: "You've already optimized everything you can optimize in English. Education is maxed. Work experience is maxed. There are only two levers left: French, and a job offer. A job offer adds 50-200 points depending on LMIA, but you don't have a Canadian employer. French is the accessible lever."

- Chris explains the TEF:
  - 4 components: reading, writing, speaking, listening
  - CLB 7 requires: NCLC 7 across all four
  - With CLB 10 English + CLB 7 French → 12 SOL points (3 per ability × 4) + 25 bilingual bonus
  - Total CRS boost: 37 points
  - 470 + 37 = 507. Above every STEM draw in history

- Amara: "So 507 is safe?"
- Chris: "Nothing is guaranteed. IRCC can change categories, change cutoffs, pause draws. But based on two years of STEM draw data, 507 would have been invited in every single STEM draw. And STEM draws have been happening every 3-6 months."
- Amara: "What's the total cost? Everything."
- Chris: "My retainer: $3,000. TEF exam fee: $400. TEF prep (Alliance Française or self-study): $200-800. Government application fees after ITA: $1,525 (processing fee $950 + right of permanent residence $575). Medical exam: ~$300. Biometrics: $85. Police certificate: ~$50. Total: approximately $5,560-6,160 CAD."
- Amara: "That's a quarter of my savings."
- Chris: "Yes. And you should know — FSWP is technically a DIY application. You don't need a consultant. The forms are online. But here's what I do that the forms don't: I make sure there's nothing in your application that triggers a request for additional documents, nothing that slows processing, nothing that creates ambiguity. One unclear work reference letter can delay you 3-4 months. At your CRS level, every draw you miss because of a document issue is a draw where someone at 477 gets invited and you don't."
- Amara: "You could have not told me I don't need you."
- Chris: "I could have. But you'd have found out on the forum in 20 minutes and you'd never trust me again."
