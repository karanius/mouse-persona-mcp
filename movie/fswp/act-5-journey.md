# Act 5 — The Journey

**Agents:** Chris + Amara
**Portals:** Consultant + Immigrant
**Duration:** Processing → COPR → landing → first job → building a life

---

## Chapter 5.1 — The Processing

### Scene 5.1.1 — Acknowledgment of Receipt (Week 1)
**Agent: Chris → Messages**
- "Amara — AOR received. IRCC has acknowledged your application. Processing clock starts now. You'll see status updates in your IRCC portal. Don't panic if nothing changes for weeks — that's normal."
- Amara: "How often should I check?"
- Chris: "Once a week. Not once a day. Watching a loading bar doesn't make it faster."
- Thinks: "He knows me."

### Scene 5.1.2 — Background Check (Months 1-3)
**Agent: Chris (periodic)**
- Nigerian passport — background check takes longer than some countries
- Chris checks IRCC processing times monthly: "FSWP currently processing in 5 months — within target"
- No requests for additional documents. File is clean
- Amara messages every 2-3 weeks: "Any update?"
- Chris: "No update means good news. If they needed something, they'd ask."
- Logs 30 minutes per check-in → retainer balance slowly decreasing

### Scene 5.1.3 — The Hardest Part
**Agent: Amara (narrated)**
- Month 3. Amara is still at Interswitch. Still building data pipelines
- She hasn't told anyone at work. If this falls through, she doesn't want pity
- She hasn't told her mother. If this works, she wants it to be a surprise
- She told only Tunde and one friend, Chioma, who is also in the Express Entry pool (CRS 463 — still waiting for a draw)
- Chioma: "I don't know how you're patient. I check IRCC six times a day."
- Amara: "I'm not patient. I'm disciplined. There's a difference."
- At night, she lies in bed and calculates: if PR is approved in month 5, she flies to Toronto in month 6. She needs a job by month 8. Her savings after everything: ~$18,000 CAD. In Toronto, that's 4-5 months of rent plus food
- She has 4-5 months of runway. That's it
- Thinks: "Tunde got hired in 6 weeks. Alok from the forum got hired in 3 weeks. But they had Canadian networks. I have one friend and a LinkedIn profile."

### Scene 5.1.4 — Pre-Arrival Networking
**Agent: Chris → Messages**
- Month 4. Chris sends an unexpected message:
- "Amara — while we wait, let's talk about landing strategy. I know this isn't in the retainer scope, but I've seen too many FSW clients arrive with approved PR and no plan. Your skills are in high demand in Toronto — data engineers with Kafka/Spark experience are needed at every major bank and fintech. Start doing three things now: 1) Update your LinkedIn to say 'Relocating to Toronto — available [month]'. 2) Connect with Nigerian tech professionals in Toronto — there's a community called NaijaCoders TO, 500+ members. 3) Apply to jobs now. Many Canadian employers will interview you remotely if you have confirmed PR."
- Amara: "You didn't have to tell me that."
- Chris: "I told you — FSWP is how practices are built. If you land with a plan, you succeed. If you succeed, you tell people. I'd rather have 50 FSWP clients at $3,000 than 5 startup clients at $15,000."
- Amara joins NaijaCoders TO on LinkedIn. 487 members. Half of them are data engineers
- She messages 3 people who work at Canadian fintechs: "Hi, I'm Amara. I'm a data engineer at Interswitch, relocating to Toronto with PR. Would love to connect."
- 2 of 3 respond within 24 hours
- Thinks: "Chris is good at this. Not just the immigration part. The landing part."

## Chapter 5.2 — Approved

### Scene 5.2.1 — Medical Passed (Month 4)
**Agent: Chris → Messages**
- "Medical results cleared. No issues. Background check appears complete — status changed to 'Decision made' in the portal."
- Amara: "Decision made? What does that mean?"
- Chris: "It means they've made a decision. In Express Entry, 'decision made' almost always means approval. You'll get the COPR letter in 1-3 weeks."
- Amara: "Almost always?"
- Chris: "In 4 years of practice, every clean file that reached 'decision made' was approved. Yours is a clean file."

### Scene 5.2.2 — COPR (Month 5)
**Agent: Chris**
- IRCC email to Amara: **Confirmation of Permanent Residence**
- PR approved
- COPR letter issued — valid for travel to Canada
- Amara must land within 12 months of the medical exam date
- Chris messages: "Amara — PR APPROVED. Congratulations. You are a permanent resident of Canada. COPR letter is in your IRCC portal. You need to land within 12 months, but I recommend arriving within 2-3 months while the job market momentum you've built is fresh. When you arrive at the airport, present your COPR and passport. They'll stamp it and you're in."
- Logs 1.5 hours final review → retainer balance: $625

### Scene 5.2.3 — Amara Tells Her Mother
**Agent: Amara (narrated)**
- Saturday morning. Amara takes a bus from Lagos to Enugu. 6 hours
- She arrives at her mother's house — the house with the leaky roof she grew up in
- Ngozi is in the kitchen making jollof rice
- Amara sits at the kitchen table
- "Mama, I need to tell you something."
- Ngozi looks at her. Studies her face. Mothers know.
- "You're leaving."
- "I got permanent residence in Canada. I'm moving to Toronto."
- Ngozi is quiet for a long time. She puts down the spoon
- "Your father always said you would go far. He didn't mean this far."
- Amara: "I'll send more money, Mama. Much more. And when Emeka finishes his residency, I'll bring him too."
- Ngozi: "I don't want more money. I want you to be safe."
- "I will be safe."
- Ngozi hugs her daughter. She doesn't let go for a long time
- Thinks: "The hardest part of immigration isn't the CRS score. It's the kitchen table."

## Chapter 5.3 — Landing

### Scene 5.3.1 — Last Day at Interswitch
**Agent: Amara (narrated)**
- Amara gives 4 weeks' notice
- Her manager, Kunle: "Where are you going? A competitor?"
- "Canada."
- Kunle: "Ah. We'll lose a good one." He shakes her hand. "Build something great."
- Her team throws a small party. Suya and Chapman's on the 11th floor
- She hands off her fraud detection pipeline to Femi, the most capable junior
- Last commit message: "Final pipeline optimization — handoff to @femi.adebayo"
- She logs off her Interswitch laptop for the last time
- Thinks: "40 million users will never know that the woman who kept their transactions safe just left."

### Scene 5.3.2 — The Flight
**Agent: Amara (narrated)**
- Lagos Murtala Muhammed Airport → Toronto Pearson. Air Canada via Montreal
- 12 hours. She has a window seat
- She watches the Sahara pass below. Then the Atlantic. Then Newfoundland — snow and forests
- She opens her phone. The CanaDREAMERS portal shows: "Congratulations, Amara. Your journey is almost complete."
- She closes it and looks out the window
- Thinks: "Every number led to this. CRS 470, then 507. STEM cutoff 488. Eleven points that became thirty-seven because a French teacher in Enugu cared about pronunciation."

### Scene 5.3.3 — Arrival at Pearson
**Agent: Amara (narrated)**
- Toronto Pearson International Airport. Terminal 1. Immigration Hall
- She joins the "New Immigrants" line
- The CBSA officer reads her COPR letter, scans her passport
- "Welcome to Canada, Ms. Okafor. You're a permanent resident."
- He stamps her COPR. Hands it back
- She walks through the doors into the arrivals hall
- Tunde is there. Holding a sign that says "WELCOME HOME, AMARA" in green and white
- She laughs. She almost cries. She doesn't cry
- Tunde: "How was the flight?"
- Amara: "Long. Let's go. I have a job interview on Monday."
- It's February. Toronto is -8°C. She's wearing a coat she bought on Jumia for ₦45,000. It's not warm enough
- Thinks: "This cold is extraordinary."

## Chapter 5.4 — Building a Life

### Scene 5.4.1 — The First Month
**Agent: Amara (narrated)**
- Amara stays with Tunde for 2 weeks in his Liberty Village condo
- She gets a SIN number on day 2 (Service Canada office at Queen and Spadina)
- Opens a bank account at TD (Bloor and Bay branch)
- Transfers remaining savings: $18,200 CAD
- Applies for OHIP (health card) — immediate coverage for new permanent residents
- Buys a proper winter coat at Uniqlo ($200)
- Applies to 14 data engineering positions in the first week
- Gets 3 callbacks. 2 from fintechs, 1 from a bank
- NaijaCoders TO monthly meetup at a WeWork in King West — she meets 30 Nigerian tech professionals, 8 are data engineers
- A woman named Ifunanya from Interswitch's Nairobi office recognizes her: "Amara? From the fraud detection team? You built the streaming migration!"
- Ifunanya works at Wealthsimple now. "We're hiring a senior data engineer. I'll refer you."

### Scene 5.4.2 — The Interview
**Agent: Amara (narrated)**
- Week 3. Video call with Wealthsimple's data platform team
- Technical interview: Design a real-time fraud detection pipeline for investment transactions
- Amara: "This is literally what I built at Interswitch. 2 million transactions a day, Kafka to Spark Structured Streaming, feature store for ML models, sub-100ms alerting."
- The interviewer pauses: "You've done this at scale?"
- "At Interswitch, the system I built processes transactions for 40 million users. Your user base is 3 million. The architecture is the same — the scale is actually simpler."
- On-site interview the following week. System design, coding, behavioral
- She walks through Liberty Village after the on-site. It's snowing. She still doesn't have proper boots
- Thinks: "I wasn't nervous. For the first time in a year, I wasn't waiting for someone else's decision. This part — the engineering part — I know I'm good at this."

### Scene 5.4.3 — The Offer
**Agent: Amara (narrated)**
- Week 5 in Canada. 6 weeks after COPR
- Email from Wealthsimple HR: "We're pleased to offer you the position of Senior Data Engineer"
- Salary: $135,000 CAD + equity + benefits
- Start date: March 15
- Amara reads the number three times. $135,000
- Her Interswitch salary was $16,000
- She calculates: after taxes and rent, she can send her mother $2,000/month. That's more than her mother earned in a year as a schoolteacher
- She messages Chris: "Got the job. Wealthsimple. $135K."
- Chris: "I knew you would. Congratulations, Amara."
- She calls her mother: "Mama, I got a job. A good job."
- Ngozi: "I knew you would. Are you eating enough?"
- "Yes, Mama."

### Scene 5.4.4 — Chris Updates
**Agent: Chris**
- Updates case: "Amara Okafor — landed, employed, settled"
- Messages to portal: "PR confirmed, employment secured at Wealthsimple (fintech), Toronto"
- Logs final 30 minutes → retainer balance: $500
- Final invoice → close case
