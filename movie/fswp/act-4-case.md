## Chapter 4.1 — The French

### Scene 4.1.1 — Preparation Begins
**Agent: Amara (narrated)**
- Amara enrolls in an intensive TEF prep course at Alliance Française de Lagos
- 3 evenings per week + Saturday mornings
- Her classmates are mostly Nigerian professionals with the same plan: boost CRS with French
- The instructor, Madame Cécile, is from Côte d'Ivoire
- First class: Amara can understand 60% of the spoken French. Her reading is stronger. Her writing is rusty. Her speaking is hesitant
- Madame Cécile: "Your grammar foundation is solid. Your vocabulary is frozen in 2010. We need to thaw it."
- Thinks: "I haven't spoken French since I was 17. Monsieur Nwosu would be proud that his classes are saving my life."

### Scene 4.1.2 — The Routine
**Agent: Amara (narrated)**
- For 3 months, Amara's life is: Interswitch 8 AM - 6 PM → Alliance Française 7 PM - 9 PM → French podcasts on the bus home → practice exercises before bed
- She downloads TV5Monde and RFI apps. Listens to French news during her commute
- She texts Madame Cécile practice paragraphs at midnight
- Her Kafka dashboards at work still hum with 2 million transactions. Nobody at Interswitch knows she's leaving
- Chris checks in monthly: "How's the French?"
- Amara: "Lentement mais sûrement." (Slowly but surely.)
- Chris: "That's CLB 7 speaking right there."

### Scene 4.1.3 — The TEF Exam
**Agent: Amara (narrated)**
- Month 4. TEF exam at Alliance Française de Lagos
- 4 sections, 3 hours total:
  - Compréhension écrite (reading): She reads a passage about renewable energy policy. Understands 80%
  - Compréhension orale (listening): A conversation between two colleagues about a project deadline. She catches the key details
  - Expression écrite (writing): Write an email to a landlord about a maintenance issue. She writes carefully, simple sentences, correct grammar
  - Expression orale (speaking): Describe your work and why you want to learn French. She speaks slowly, makes two verb conjugation errors, but communicates clearly
- Results come 3 weeks later
- Scores: NCLC 8 (reading), NCLC 7 (listening), NCLC 7 (writing), NCLC 7 (speaking)
- All four components at NCLC 7 or above → CLB 7 French ✅
- Thinks: "Merci, Monsieur Nwosu. Merci, Madame Cécile. Merci, Lagos traffic for giving me 2 hours a day to listen to RFI."

### Scene 4.1.4 — The New CRS
**Agent: Chris**
- TEF results uploaded to Amara's profile
- CRS recalculation:
  - Previous: 470
  - SOL points (CLB 7 French, 3 per ability × 4): +12
  - Bilingual bonus (CLB 10 English + CLB 7+ French): +25
  - New CRS: **507**
- Chris messages: "Amara — 507. The highest STEM cutoff in history was 491. You're 16 points above that. You're in range for every STEM draw that has ever happened."
- Amara: "When is the next one?"
- Chris: "We don't know. They've been every 3-6 months. The last one was 5 months ago. It could be next week. It could be next month. We submit your Express Entry profile now and we wait."
- Logs 2 hours for TEF review + CRS optimization → retainer balance: $2,500

### Scene 4.2.2 — Chris Explains the Wait
**Agent: Chris → Messages**
- "Amara, you're in the pool. CRS 507. Now comes the part I can't control: the draw. IRCC conducts Express Entry draws roughly every 2 weeks. But STEM-specific draws are less frequent — usually every 3-6 months. When a STEM draw happens, they set a CRS cutoff and invite everyone above it. Your 507 has been above every STEM cutoff since 2023. The median wait time for STEM draws is about 4 months from profile submission. I'll message you every time a draw happens — even if it's not STEM."
- Amara: "So I just wait?"
- Chris: "You wait. But you prepare. Start gathering the full application documents now — passport copies, photos, detailed work history, additional reference letters. When the ITA comes, you have 60 days to submit. We should be ready in 2 weeks, not scrambling for 60 days."
